Sauce

Description: This sauce is sweet, savoury, spicy, and a little pungent. It's delicious with rice, veggies, meat, and noodles. I absolutely adore it! Source: from a recipe on allrecipes.com, by "Calamity in the kitchen"

Ingredients

This recipe is very simple, but the resutls are amazing.
It goes very well with this. I fell in love with this sauce while eating at Vietnamese restaurants. Particularly at Pho place. This sauce is served with meats and spring rolls, as well as vermecelli noodle salads. Those vermecelli noodle salads are what really got me to fall in love with this sauce.

  • Sugar
  • Water
  • Fish sauce
  • Sliced Thai bird's eye chiles
  • Minced garlic
  • Lime
  1. This recipe is very simple. Follow this following ratio for the sugar, water, and fish sauce. 1:5:1 That is, one part sugar, five parts water, and one part fish sauce. I've seen some recipes call for warm water, but I haven't had any trouble with just room temperature water. It shouldn't hurt though! So, for example, you could have 1 tbsp sugar, 5 tbsp water, and 1 tbsp fish sauce. Adjust according to taste, as always.
  2. Add lime juice, minced garlic, and sliced Thai bird's eye chiles. Add these all to taste. Be careful with the chiles, as they can be very hot. I really like the flavour that they add, but the heat can be overwhelming! You can try to get the seeds out to reduce the heat. Also, remember if you touched these chiles with your hands, DO NOT touch your face or any other sensitive areas.
